## 📖 Teaching Advice for Software Engineering Project
The teaching advice provides an overview of each focus area in Year 12 and additional advice regarding the planning and delivery of the content to maximise student synthesis.

### 🤔 Why is it important?
The Software Engineering Project is to be completed in Year 12 and will require students to develop a project of their choice based on the knowledge and skills developed in Year 11 and Year 12.

Students have an opportunity to develop deeper knowledge, understanding and skills in an area of interest as they engage in a software engineering project that addresses an identified real-world problem. Students refine and apply project-management skills as they develop, document, implement and evaluate their project solution. Learning in this focus area emphasises project-management and software development approaches, explores the tools used by software designers and engineers, and explores the concept of delivering solutions for a client. Highly valued industry skills such as collaboration and communication are developed through project work where students consider involving and empowering clients, responding to stakeholders, and negotiating with peers.

Teachers are encouraged to integrate the learning content of this focus area within all other Year 12 focus areas.
